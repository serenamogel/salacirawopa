Who Are Kevin Bieksa Kids? Details To Know About The Family Of The Ice Hockey Defenceman

Posted by Martina Birk on Saturday, June 29, 2024

Former Canadian ice hockey defenceman has two children, a son and a daughter, whose names have yet to be revealed. Let’s learn more about the Ice Hockey Defenceman’s family.

Kevin Bieksa most recently played for the National Hockey League’s Anaheim Ducks.

Bieksa received a scholarship to Bowling Green State University after a three-year career with the Burlington Cougars in the Ontario Junior Hockey League.

Is Kevin Bieksa Featured On Wikipedia?

Kevin Bieksa’s bio can be found on Wikipedia’s official page, and it includes information about his playing career, playing style, and personal life.

He was born on June 16, 1981, in Grimsby, Canada, and will turn 40 on October 24, 2021.

During his four years with the Falcons of the Central Collegiate Hockey Association, he was named to the All-CCHA Honorable Mention team once.

He earned a bachelor’s degree in finance from the institution and was a two-time CCHA All-Academic honorable mention in 2003 and 2004. Hockey Night in Canada now has Bieksa as a co-host.

Find Out Kevin’s Kids & Wife

Bieksa is married to Katie, and they have two children, a son and a daughter. More information regarding his kid’s name is yet to be published.

Newport Beach, California, is where the family calls home. Katie’s book, Newport Jane, was released in 2017.

His father is a member of the Ontario Federation of Labour and a minor hockey coach for his three sons. Marty and Bryan are his brothers, and Terri Lynn and Jennifer are his stepsisters.

Who Is Kevin Bieksa?

Kevin Bieksa is a former Canadian Ice Hockey Defenceman. He was designated team captain and one of Canada’s top three players in the event.

With the Canucks, Bieksa was recognized as a two-way defenseman.

Coaches and NHL officials complimented him for his leadership qualities throughout his career.

Bieksa competed in the 2014 World Hockey Championships in Minsk, Belarus, for his country.
