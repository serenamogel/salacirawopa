The Life and Death Story of Anabel Gutierrez: Biography,Net Worth,Age,&amp; more

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Saturday, September 7, 2024

Anabel Gutierrez was a Mexican actress and comedian known profoundly for her role in the film ‘School for Tramps’ that premiered in 1955. She was remembered for her appearances on Chespirito, a program in which she played the character of Doña Espotaverderona in some sketches. It is sad as actress and comedian will no longer be on our screens after she was reported dead on Sunday, August 21, 2022, in Puebla, Mexico. Her demise comes just a few weeks to her 91st birthday.

Who was Anabel Gutierrez?

Anabel Gutierrez was a Mexican movie star who was born September 5, 1931 and she passed away on August 21, 2022, in State of Puebla, Mexico. There seem to be no information about her birth parents, siblings and educational background. All that’s in the known is that she’s the mother of actress Amairani Romero Gutiérrez and the grandmother to Amairani’s daughter Macarena Garcia. We are yet to confirm whether or not she was married as of her time of death.

In 1949, Anabel made short stints in the films El Diablo no es Tan Diablo and La liga de las muchachas, coming in as an extra. After her performances from those two films, she gained recognition and began playing major roles. One of the first films in which she played a major role was in Deseada, a 1950 film. She acted opposite to Dolores del Río in an older sister / younger sister love triangle with Jorge Mistral.

Her great performances got her nominated in 5 categories for the Ariel Awards and eventually winning the Best Musical Score. Same year, she starred in Azahares para tu boda with Fernando Soler, Marga Lopez, Sara Garcia and Joaquin Pardavé. After her ground breaking acting skills caught people’s attention she was successive roles in other movies.

Her most notable ones were Muchachas de uniforme (1951), Rostros olvidados (1952), and Escuela de vagabundos (1954). Those three movies got her nominations for Best Youth Actor with Ariel Award. She did win the prestigious award with her 1954 movie, Escuela de vagabundos.

In the late 1960s, Gutiérrez began working in television and developed a working relationship with Gómez Bolaños Roberto that would bring her second fame. Her first series with him was in El Ciudadano Gómez but the work that made her an icon is Doña Espotaverderona, the mother of La Chimoltrufia, in the television program.

Gutiérrez was the mother of actress Amairani Romero Gutiérrez popularly called Amairani. She passed away on August 21, 2022, just a couple more weeks to her 91st birthday. Anabel was involved in over 30 movies with a further 19 television series in her career. She is said to have an estimated net worth of US$2 million. Her source of income is mainly from acting.
