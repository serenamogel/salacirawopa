Career

Meghan Trainor started her career as a songwriter and performer. She wrote her first original song titled Give Me a Chance when she was just 13 years old. After that, she records and produce her compositions with the help of a production house named Logic Studio and later worked independently in a home studio.

From the year 2009 to 2013, she has released three albums which she had written, produced, recorded, and performed. In the year 2009, she was enrolled in the Summer Performance Program at the Berklee College of Music and released her debut album titled Meghan Trainor.

In the year 2010, she released her debut song titled Take Care of Our Soldiers, and the very next year she released the acoustic albums titled I’ll Sing with You and Only 17. In the year 2012, she signed a deal with Big Yellow Dog Music and decided to pursue her career as a songwriter-for-hire.

She has also released her song titled Who I Wanna Be in the same year. In the year 2013, she traveled to Nashville, New York City, and Los Angeles where she sang lead and background vocals for several other artists occasionally. In the same year, she co-wrote a song titled In the Sun. Later in the year, she moved to Nashville and wrote songs for artists like Hunter Hayes, Rascal Flatts, R5, and Sabrina Carpenter.

In the year 2014, she co-wrote the song titled All About That Bass and released her debut extended play titled Title. Later in the same year, she has been featured as a guest mentor in the popular television show titled The Voice Season 7 and released her second single titled Lips Are Movin. In the year 2015, she released another album titled Title same as her extended play with singles like Dear Future Husband and Like I’m Gonna Lose You.

In the same year, she began her first headlining concert tour titled That Bass Tour and has been featured as a musical guest in the third season of the popular television reality show titled Undateable. She has also been featured in the song titled Boys Like You by Who is Fancy along with Ariana Grande.
