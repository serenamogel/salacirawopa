Who Is Troy Closson New York Times Journalist? Meet Him On Twitter

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Wednesday, October 9, 2024

Who is Troy Closson? He is the New York Times Journalist who has more than a decade-long experience in the field. 

On the Metro desk in New York, Troy Closson covers law enforcement and criminal justice.

Troy attended Northwestern University. He graduated with the degree in bachelor of science (BS), Journalism.

Learn everything there’s about Troy Closson through the article here.

Troy Closson Wiki & Bio

As part of the fellowship program, Troy Closson joined The New York Times in June 2020. According to his Linkedin profile, Troy worked as reporting fellow from June 2020 to April 2021, which means he did the role for 11 months.

Later Troy was promoted to a full-time metro reporter in New York Times from April 2021 until now. He has been doing the role for more than eight months now.

He formerly worked for The Texas Tribune, the Chicago Sun-Times, and Block Club Chicago and is a Maryland native.

Troy Closson was the Editor-in-Chief of The Daily Northwestern at Northwestern University. He worked as an intern at The Texas Tribune, The Chicago Sun-Times, and Block Club Chicago.

Troy most recently worked for ProPublica as an Emerging Reporter.

Meet Troy Closson on Twitter

Troy Closson is pretty active on Twitter. Per his Twitter account, he has put a bio, “watching blake griffin & writing about new york.”

Moreover, he joined Twitter in December 2018. According to his Twitter profile, Troy is based in Brooklyn, New York.

You can find him on Twitter by the username @troy_closson. As of November 2021, Troy has accumulated more than 3000 followers on there.

He is frequently found retweeting breaking news on his Twitter accounts as a reporter. In addition, Troy ensures that his followers are kept up to date on current events.
