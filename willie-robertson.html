Willie Robertson [Duck Dynasty] net worth, Wiki, Bio, wife, kids, adoption, career, facts.

Posted by Martina Birk on Sunday, June 23, 2024

When Phil Robertson decided to step down from his job as the CEO of The Duck Commander, he left quite a large void to be filled. His legacy was so large that it was almost impossible for someone to come in and take his place. As the leadership changed hands from Phil to his son Willie, many were not so sure about it. But over the years, Willie has taken on all the challenges that were set out to him and has surpassed the expectations of many. In this article, we will take a closer look at the wiki-bio of Willie Robertson.

Willie Robertson is an American TV personality, businessman, author, active outdoorsman, speaker and news contributor. He is best known for his appearances on the reality TV series Duck Dynasty on A&E, which followed his family’s duck call business, Duck Commander, of which he is the current CEO. He is also the executive producer of Buck Commander, another hunting-related show on the Outdoor Channel. He lives in West Monroe, Louisiana with his wife Korie and his six children: John Luke, Sadie, Will, Rowdy, Bella, and Rebecca.

Net Worth

Willie Robertson’s net worth is estimated to be around $45 million as of May 2023. Willie Robertson’s main source of income is from being the CEO of Duck Commander, which is a company that sells duck calls and other hunting gear. He also earns money from being the executive producer and star of Duck Dynasty, which was a reality TV series that aired on A&E from 2012 to 2017. Additionally, he owns other businesses such as Buck Commander, which is a subsidiary of Duck Commander that sells deer hunting products, and Willie’s Duck Diner, which is a restaurant that serves Southern cuisine.

Early Life:

Willie Jess Robertson was born on the 22nd of April 1972 to Phil and Marsha Kay Robertson. The third child of the pair, Willie has two older brothers, Alan and Jase, one younger sister, Phyllis, and one younger brother, Jep. Like his father, Willie was into hunting from a young age and accompanied his father to the winds. Of all the Robertson children, Willie was the one who was most interested in the family business and spent a considerable amount of time learning about duck calling and hunting. After graduating from a local high school, Willie enrolled at Harding University but transferred to NE Louisiana University, at a later date. He graduated in 2014, with a business degree.

Professional Career:

Right after graduating from University, Will made the trip back to his hometown and began assisting his father in running the business. His business degree proved to be an asset to the company, and Phil left the leadership position to his son. After taking over his father, Will expanded the family business from a living room operation to a multi-million-dollar enterprise and destination for all things outdoors. This growth helped the Robertsons to secure a reality TV show, Duck Dynasty.

While Will is most known as the CEO of the Duck Commander, he is also the founder and CEO of a subsidiary company Buck Commander. The company is also at the center of a reality TV series under the same name produced by Under Armour on the Outdoor Channel. In the longlist of his business ventures, Willie also allows visits to his museum which is a museum in West Monroe, Louisiana under “Duck Commander The Tour.”

His other business includes 4 Bears Music Group, Robertson Brothers Farm, WK Properties, Back 40 Properties, Commander Services Co, Commerce Street Holdings, WKR Investments, and more.

Television:

Over the years, Will appeared on several television shows related to his family business and his personal beliefs. He appeared in Duck Commander from 2009 to 2011, which became the base for the new show, Duck Dynasty. Will stayed for the entire run of the series from 2012 to 2017. Will also appeared in the series Buck Commander from 2010 to the present.

He has also made cameo appearances on other shows, such as Last Man Standing and the Christian film God’s Not Dead, with his wife. He has also been a guest of United States Representative Vance McAllister at the 2014 State of the Union Address by President Barack Obama. He has also been interviewed by Conan O’Brien on his show CONAN. He has also produced a Christian Broadway-style play called His Story.
