Career

Fuller started 12 of 13 games as a true freshman in 2013, totaling 58 tackles and six interceptions. He was named the ACC Defensive Rookie of the Year for his efforts. In 2014, Fuller returned as a starter during his sophomore season. Fuller declared for the 2016 NFL Draft on December 15, 2015. Due to a torn meniscus suffered in the fall, Fuller was unable to participate in any physical exercises at the NFL Scouting Combine. Fuller was anticipated to be a first-round choice by NFL draft experts and scouts before tearing his meniscus. DraftScout.com ranked Fuller as the fifth-best cornerback prospect in the draft, NFL expert Mike Mayock ranked him as the ninth-best cornerback, and Sports Illustrated listed him as the tenth greatest defensive back prospect.
