Who is Chau Nguyen (News Anchor)? | Chau Nguyen Biography-Wiki

Chau Nguyen is an American former Vietnamese-American news anchor most recently seen with KHOU-TV, prior to stepping down in December 2007. Starting in 2003, she served as a morning news anchor at KHOU in her native Houston, Texas.

The Houston Chronicle website reported that she gave notice as well as quit her job at KHOU in late 2007. It was reported that she travels the world for several months prior to returning home, to find work in a field outside of television journalism.

She was chosen by the Vietnamese American National Gala (VANG) to be the Master of Ceremonies in the year2007. She was named “Best Reporter” by the Houston Press in the same year. Nguyen anchored her last Saturday morning broadcast for KHOU in December 2007. Nguyen walked away on her terms because she wanted a change: “That’s 15 years in TV news according to her blog.

I’m ready to pursue a new challenge. I loved working in TV, and I’ll miss the friends I’ve made here, but I want a normal life. I don’t want to work weekends. I want holidays off. I want to take Christmas off. TV news doesn’t fit who I am now. I’m not a glamour girl. I’m an introspective person. Fifteen years is a long time to do only one thing.”

She went back to graduate school to pursue her Masters in Social Work from the University of Houston. She graduated in May 2010. Nguyen then started pursuing a new career as a social worker as well as a psychotherapist.

Since then, Chau has appeared several times on a number of local news stations to offer her therapeutic expertise about children as well as teens.

Chau accepted a position at The Women’s Home as a Manager of Community Involvement in 2013. Nguyen has made several public appearances, often working as a spokesperson for the organization in her role.

Chau was the Mistress of Ceremony at the 2016 City of Houston Mayor as well as Elected Officials Inauguration at downtown’s Jones Hall in January 2016. Nguyen was named Chief Marketing Officer at the Houston Area Women’s Center (www.hawc.org)in April 2016.
