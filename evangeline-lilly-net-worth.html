Evangeline Lily | Career

A Ford Modelling Agency agent spotted Lilly while she was in Kelowna, British Columbia.

She accepted the agent’s business card but did not pursue acting right away.

Evangeline ultimately contacted the agency, and she was cast in various advertisements and non-speaking roles in the television shows “Smallville” and “Kingdom Hospital.”

Despite the fact that most of her TV appearances were as extras, she became an overnight sensation when she landed the role of Kate Austen in the series “Lost.”

Lilly was encouraged by a friend to audition for the part, but she had not expected to be cast.

Seventy-five actresses auditioned for the role of Kate Austen, and writer and co-creator Damon Lindelof claimed that when he and J.J. Abrams watched Lilly’s audition tape, they knew they had their Kate right away.

Similarly, she starred in the thriller “Afterwards” in 2008 and then took a break from acting in 2010 to focus on authoring children’s books.
