Kim Collins (born April 5, 1976) is a Saint Kitts and Nevis sprinter who competes in track and field events. In the 100 metre dash, he won the World Championship in 200From 1996 through 2008, he represented his nation in the Summer Olympics on four separate times, and he was the first athlete from his country to ever reach a final in an Olympic sport. His other accomplishments include competing in eight straight World Championships, starting in 1997 and continuing until 201Collins was dropped from the Saint Kitts and Nevis Olympic team before the 100m heats at the London 2012 Olympic Games after a disagreement with his country’s Olympic officials over an unapproved meeting with his wife in a London hotel. 

Collins was a member of the Saint Kitts and Nevis national team before the 2008 Beijing Olympics. Collins won the bronze medal in the 200 m at the 2001 World Championships in Athletics and went on to win the 100 m title at the 2002 Commonwealth Games with a time of 9.98 seconds, breaking the ten-second barrier. Collins has also competed in the 400 m and the 800 m. He earned a silver medal in the 60-meter dash at the 2003 IAAF World Indoor Championships before going on to win the title in the event at the 2004 IAAF World Outdoor Championships. Aside from that, he has also won sprint medals at the World Championships in 2005, the 2008 IAAF World Indoor Championships, and the World Championships in 201On the 4th of July in Lausanne, Switzerland, during the diamond league, he ran a time of 9.97 seconds to establish a new personal and national record in the 100 metres.

On July 10, 2013, he broke the ten-second barrier for the sixth time, recording a time of 9.99 in Budapest.
